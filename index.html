Day 1: Friday, May 16th

Theme: Arrival & Ghoom Exploration

Map: Route from Bagdogra Airport (IXB) to Tigerhill Homestay (Jorebunglow/Ghoom). Highlight Ghoom Monastery, Ghoom Station, Batasia Loop in proximity to the homestay.

~11:05 AM - 3:00 PM

Arrival at Bagdogra (IXB) & Transfer to Ghoom

  • Meet your pre-booked driver at the airport arrivals.
  • The drive typically takes 3-4 hours. Be patient, enjoy the views!
  • Road conditions and traffic can affect travel time.
~3:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Hotel Check-in (Tigerhill Homestay) & Refresh

  • Check into Tigerhill Homestay, Jorebunglow.
  • Take some time to freshen up and perhaps have a late lunch or snack.
~4:00 PM - 6:00 PM

Explore Ghoom Locally

  • Ghoom Monastery (Yiga Choeling):

    Historic monastery known for its large Maitreya Buddha statue and collection of scriptures.

    Review: Generally positive reviews, appreciated for its spiritual ambiance and historical significance.

  • Ghoom Railway Station (UNESCO):

    Highest railway station in India, part of the DHR World Heritage site.

    Review: Highly rated for its heritage value and connection to the Toy Train.

  • Batasia Loop & War Memorial:

    A scenic railway loop with panoramic views and a memorial honoring Gorkha soldiers.

    Review: Very popular for photography and views (weather dependent); memorial adds poignancy.

  • Dress modestly for the monastery.
  • Walk slowly to acclimatize.
  • Check views at Batasia Loop.
  • Observe prayer rituals.
  • Take photos.
  • Reflect at the War Memorial.
Evening

Dinner & Rest

  • Find a local restaurant in Ghoom/Jorebunglow.
  • Get adequate rest for acclimatization.

Day 2: Saturday, May 17th

Theme: Darjeeling Town & Toy Train Fun!

Map: Route from Ghoom/Jorebunglow to Darjeeling Town. Highlight HMI/Zoo, Darjeeling Railway Station, and Chowrasta (Mall Road).

~9:00 AM - 12:30 PM

HMI & Zoo Visit

  • Himalayan Mountaineering Institute (HMI):

    Prestigious institute with a museum detailing mountaineering history, Everest expeditions, and equipment.

    Review: Highly rated, especially for those interested in mountaineering and Himalayan history.

  • Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park (Zoo):

    Specializes in high-altitude animals; known for successful Red Panda and Snow Leopard conservation programs.

    Review: Very popular, considered one of the best zoos in India for its focus on Himalayan fauna and conservation.

  • Allow 3+ hours for both (same complex).
  • Wear comfortable shoes.
  • Typically closed Thursdays (confirm).
  • Learn mountaineering history.
  • Spot Red Pandas, Snow Leopards.
  • Support conservation.
~12:30 PM - 1:15 PM

Lunch in Darjeeling

  • Choose restaurant near station/Mall Road.
  • Variety of cuisines available.
~1:15 PM - 3:30 PM

Toy Train Joy Ride (Pre-Booked 1:25 PM)

  • Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (Toy Train):

    Iconic UNESCO World Heritage narrow-gauge railway offering scenic rides.

    Review: Highly recommended for the unique heritage experience and views, though can be slow.

  • Batasia Loop stop:

    Brief stop on the Joy Ride for photos at the scenic loop and memorial.

  • Be at Darjeeling station by 1:15 PM.
  • Have booking details.
  • Enjoy the ride & views!
  • Photography.
~3:30 PM onwards

Explore Darjeeling Mall (Chowrasta)

  • Darjeeling Mall (Chowrasta):

    The main public square and promenade, lined with shops, cafes, and benches. The social hub of Darjeeling.

    Review: Essential Darjeeling experience; great for people-watching, atmosphere, and evening strolls.

  • Ideal for walking (vehicle restricted).
  • Can be busy.
  • Stroll, browse shops.
  • **Try Momos!**
  • Enjoy tea/coffee.
  • People-watch.
Evening

Dinner

  • Options in Darjeeling or return to Ghoom/Jorebunglow.

Day 3: Sunday, May 18th

Theme: Sunrise Views & Spiritual Serenity

Map: Route from Ghoom/Jorebunglow to Tiger Hill (and back). Also show locations of Japanese Peace Pagoda, a representative Tea Estate (e.g., Happy Valley), and Tibetan Refugee Centre relative to Ghoom/Darjeeling.

~3:45 AM - 7:00 AM

Tiger Hill Sunrise Attempt (Weather Dependent)

  • Tiger Hill Viewpoint:

    Famous for panoramic sunrise views over Kanchenjunga and other Himalayan peaks.

    Review: Absolutely stunning when clear, but highly weather-dependent and often very crowded.

  • Highly weather dependent!
  • Leave Ghoom/Jorebunglow ~4:00 AM.
  • **Dress very warmly!**
  • Expect crowds, potential fees.
  • Skip if weather is bad.
  • Find good viewing spot.
  • Photography.
  • Sip hot tea.
Morning

Breakfast & Relax

  • Return to hotel after sunrise/wake up.
  • Breakfast included at Tigerhill Homestay.
Late Morning / Afternoon

Japanese Peace Pagoda & Temple Visit

  • Japanese Peace Pagoda:

    A large white stupa built to promote world peace, offering serene atmosphere and views.

    Review: Highly rated for its peaceful ambiance, architectural beauty, and panoramic views (weather permitting).

  • Nipponzan Myohoji Temple:

    Adjacent traditional Japanese Buddhist temple.

  • Maintain quiet, respectful atmosphere.
  • Remove shoes at temple.
  • Walk around the stupa.
  • Observe carvings.
  • Enjoy tranquility and views.
  • Attend prayer session (check timings).
Afternoon

Tea Estate Visit & Tibetan Refugee Centre

  • Tea Estate (e.g., Happy Valley):

    Visit a working tea garden to see tea bushes and learn about the famous Darjeeling tea production.

    Review: Generally well-regarded; experience varies by estate and whether factory tours/tastings are available.

  • Tibetan Refugee Self Help Centre:

    Supports the Tibetan community; watch artisans create carpets, woodcrafts, etc., and purchase items.

    Review: Valued for its cultural significance and opportunity to buy authentic crafts, though workshop access may vary.

  • Check tea factory tour timings.
  • **CRITICAL: Tibetan Centre likely CLOSED Sunday - Verify & Swap if needed!**
  • Take tea tour/tasting.
  • Buy fresh tea.
  • Support Tibetan artisans.
Evening

Relax / Dinner

  • Dinner in Ghoom/Jorebunglow or Darjeeling.

Day 4: Monday, May 19th

Theme: Nature Excursion & Last Souvenirs

Map: Route from Ghoom/Jorebunglow to Rock Garden/Ganga Maya Park (likely via Darjeeling Town). Also indicate general area of Darjeeling Town for shopping.

Morning

Rock Garden & Ganga Maya Park OR Missed Spots

  • Rock Garden:

    Multi-level terraced garden built around a natural waterfall, involving a steep drive down.

    Review: Appreciated for its unique construction and scenic setting, though requires some walking.

  • Ganga Maya Park:

    Located below Rock Garden, featuring flower gardens, a lake (boating sometimes available), and occasional folk dances.

    Review: Pleasant addition to Rock Garden visit, offers a more relaxed environment.

  • Alternative:** Cover missed spots (HMI/Zoo, Tea Estate, Peace Pagoda, Tibetan Centre if open).
  • Steep drive down.
  • Involves walking/steps.
  • Allow 2-3 hrs including travel.
  • Enjoy nature, photography.
  • Relax by waterfall/lake.
Afternoon

Lunch & Last-Minute Shopping

  • Visit Darjeeling town shops.
  • Good buys: Tea, woollens, handicrafts.
  • Compare prices, bargain politely.
  • Try different lunch spot.
  • Buy gifts and mementos.
Evening

Farewell Dinner & Packing

  • Enjoy final dinner.
  • Pack bags for early departure.
  • Confirm driver pickup time.
